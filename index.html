BirdBuoy

BirdBuoy is a student-built autonomous monitoring platform developed at Greenville University to study bird activity alongside changing environmental conditions. Our current prototype focuses on reliable field deployment, motion-triggered event capture, and practical data logging while protecting key design details during development.

Intro

BirdBuoy is an autonomous environmental monitoring system designed to observe bird activity in remote outdoor settings. The platform combines optical and environmental sensing with solar-assisted power and onboard data logging so field data can be captured with less manual observation.

As the project has evolved, our team narrowed the prototype scope to emphasize dependable operation, faster development, and stronger testability. The current system is designed for land deployment and shallow-water tripod use, with adjustable positioning to improve viewing angle and setup flexibility. That shift lets us focus on demonstrating real functionality in the prototype while preserving room for future expansion.

BirdBuoy is being developed for researchers, conservation work, and outdoor users who benefit from better insight into bird movement and the conditions surrounding it. While we share the project at a high level, some implementation details are intentionally withheld as the design continues to mature.

Our Work

Our senior design team is building and testing a working prototype that ties together power, sensing, processing, and enclosure design into one field-ready system. Recent design changes were made to improve reliability, simplify deployment, and keep the project focused on a strong first prototype.

Refined Project Scope

We narrowed the prototype to prioritize dependable performance and clearer validation. The current version emphasizes fixed mounting, adjustable aiming, and practical deployment rather than pursuing every early concept at once.

Event-Based Monitoring

The system is being built to capture targeted visual events when motion occurs, rather than recording everything continuously. This reduces storage load and makes collected data more useful during review and testing.

Environmental Data Logging

BirdBuoy pairs detected activity with environmental context such as weather and location data. That combined dataset is a major part of the project vision and helps connect bird activity to real field conditions.

Integrated Electronics

Our electronics architecture separates sensing tasks from higher-level logging and processing so the prototype can operate more efficiently. The team has been focused on integration, safe power distribution, and packaging data into readable outputs.

Autonomous Power

The prototype uses a solar-charged battery system intended for long-duration outdoor use. Current testing focuses on endurance, charge behavior, and making sure the platform can remain useful through realistic field conditions.

Testing in Progress

We are actively validating battery life, motion-trigger reliability, viewing range, environmental data accuracy, and false-trigger behavior. These tests are guiding the final tuning of the prototype before full demonstration.

Protected Development

Because BirdBuoy may support future intellectual property efforts, this website shares the project at a system level only. Specific implementation details, exact build methods, and certain design decisions are intentionally omitted.

About

About the BirdBuoy Project

BirdBuoy is a senior design project focused on creating an autonomous platform for monitoring bird activity alongside environmental conditions. The current prototype combines imaging, weather-aware sensing, onboard logging, and off-grid power concepts in a form that can be deployed outdoors for testing and future refinement.

Over the course of development, our team made several engineering tradeoffs to keep the project practical within time, budget, and manufacturing limits. That included simplifying certain earlier concepts, improving serviceability, and designing the unit around a more stable adjustable mounting approach for prototype testing.

Our Mission

Our goal is to build a practical engineering tool that helps connect bird activity with the environmental context around it. We want BirdBuoy to demonstrate how thoughtful sensing, autonomous power, and focused data collection can support future research, conservation, and informed outdoor decision-making.
