BirdBuoy

Developed by Greenville University engineering students, BirdBuoy uses cutting-edge sensors and solar power to help researchers and communities better understand bird migration and the conditions that influence it.

Intro

BirdBuoy is a battery and solar-powered portable monitoring station that tracks bird migration from either land or water. Using weather sensors, cameras, optional radar, and acoustic detection, it reveals how atmospheric conditions influence bird movement. Researchers and conservationists can use BirdBuoy to study migration patterns, while hunters and bird enthusiasts gain valuable insight into peak flight times and conditions for a variety of game birds. Beyond field use, BirdBuoy also opens the door for deeper research opportunities, from local habitat studies to large-scale migration networks. Its modular design means you can scale deployment from a single unit in a pond to a broad array covering an entire flyway, giving users flexibility to match their needs and goals.

Our Work

The BirdBuoy team is building a fully functional prototype that merges mechanical and electrical engineering into one reliable, field-ready system. Every part of the design is created to perform autonomously in real outdoor conditions while being durable, efficient, and easy to service.

Design & Planning

Our process began with defining environmental challenges such as water exposure, balance, and long-term outdoor use. Using CAD software, we created detailed 3D models to optimize buoyancy, weight distribution, and internal organization. The modular design allows for upgrades and scalability, adapting to both land and water applications.

Mechanical Fabrication

The structure is being fabricated using marine-safe materials and precision 3D-printed mounts. Each component is engineered to protect sensitive hardware while maintaining easy access for maintenance. The buoy exterior is shaped for stability and resistance to rough environmental conditions.

Electrical & Control Integration

Inside, the system includes power regulation, data collection, and environmental monitoring modules that communicate seamlessly with onboard processing. Power is supplied through a compact renewable setup designed for consistent, low-maintenance operation.

Data Collection & Processing

The onboard controller organizes and stores environmental information, allowing the team to analyze performance trends and environmental patterns during testing. The system is designed to handle continuous data flow and optimize storage efficiency.

Testing & Validation

Field and water tests will measure durability, balance, and accuracy. Results from these trials are used to refine both the mechanical structure and the internal systems to improve stability and functionality.

About

About the BirdBuoy Project

BirdBuoy is a student-engineered environmental monitoring system developed at Greenville University to study bird migration and flight patterns. The device combines atmospheric sensors, radar-style detection, and audio/visual monitoring — all powered by a compact solar-battery system. Its modular design allows for both floating and land-based operation, enabling real-time data collection on bird flight activity under varying weather conditions.
