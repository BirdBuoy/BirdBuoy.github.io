Intro

BirdBuoy is a portable, solar-assisted monitoring station designed to collect bird activity and environmental data in the field. The system is being developed to operate from either a floating buoy platform or a land-based tripod configuration, making it flexible for ponds, wetlands, refuges, and other migration corridors.

By combining weather sensing, visual imaging, thermal sensing, and acoustic monitoring, BirdBuoy helps connect bird movement to real environmental conditions. The goal is to create a practical tool for researchers, conservation groups, and outdoor users who want clearer insight into when birds are moving, what conditions influence activity, and how those patterns change over time.

Our Work

The BirdBuoy team is currently developing and refining a field-ready prototype that brings together mechanical design, electrical integration, power management, and data collection into one modular system. Recent work has focused on turning our early concept into a practical platform that can be tested outdoors and improved through real-world results.

Design Evolution

Our design has grown from an initial concept into a more defined prototype with both buoy and tripod deployment options. This allows the project to support multiple use cases while keeping the system modular for future upgrades.

Mechanical Development

We are refining the enclosure, mounting hardware, and structural layout to improve durability, serviceability, and environmental protection. Special attention is being given to stability, internal organization, and sensor placement.

Power System Integration

BirdBuoy uses a compact solar and battery architecture intended to support autonomous operation in the field. Current efforts include organizing power distribution, improving reliability, and matching energy use to the sensing schedule.

Sensor & Control Integration

The prototype combines onboard processing with environmental, audio, and imaging sensors to gather synchronized field data. This work includes wiring, communication between devices, and building a reliable data-collection workflow.

Data Strategy

Our system is being developed to record useful field data efficiently rather than storing unnecessary information. That means organizing sensor inputs, testing event-based collection ideas, and preparing for meaningful post-test analysis.

Testing & Validation

As the prototype matures, testing will focus on field durability, sensor performance, deployment stability, and overall system reliability. These results will guide the next design revisions and help validate the concept.

About

About the BirdBuoy Project

BirdBuoy is a senior design project developed at Greenville University. It brings together mechanical and electrical engineering to create a sustainable monitoring system for bird activity, migration behavior, and changing environmental conditions. The platform is designed to support both floating and land-based deployment while remaining modular for future improvements.
